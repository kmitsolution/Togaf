The roles **Solution Architect** and **Enterprise Architect** are both senior architecture positions in IT, but they differ mainly in **scope, focus, and level of abstraction**.

---

## High-Level Difference (One-Line Summary)

* **Enterprise Architect (EA)** ‚Üí Defines the **big-picture IT strategy** for the entire organization
* **Solution Architect (SA)** ‚Üí Designs **specific technical solutions** to solve particular business problems

## Responsibilities Explained

### üß© Solution Architect

Responsible for **how a solution is built**.

**Typical responsibilities:**

* Translate business requirements into technical designs
* Choose technologies, frameworks, and integration patterns
* Define system architecture (APIs, databases, cloud, security)
* Ensure performance, scalability, and reliability
* Support development and implementation teams

**Example:**
Designing a cloud-based CRM system using microservices, REST APIs, and Azure/AWS.

---

### üè¢ Enterprise Architect

Responsible for **how all solutions fit together**.

**Typical responsibilities:**

* Define enterprise architecture standards and principles
* Align IT strategy with business goals
* Decide which platforms, technologies, and vendors are allowed
* Reduce system duplication and technical debt
* Govern architecture across multiple projects

**Example:**
Deciding that the organization will standardize on AWS, SAP, and microservices across all departments.

---

## How They Work Together

* **Enterprise Architect** sets the rules and direction
* **Solution Architect** designs solutions within those rules

## Skills Comparison

### Solution Architect Skills

* System design
* Cloud & application architecture
* APIs, databases, integration
* Strong hands-on technical expertise

### Enterprise Architect Skills

* Business strategy alignment
* IT governance & standards
* Portfolio & capability modeling
* Strong stakeholder and leadership skills

---

## Which Role Is Right for You?

* Choose **Solution Architect** if you enjoy **hands-on design and problem-solving**
* Choose **Enterprise Architect** if you enjoy **strategy, governance, and long-term planning**
